Data Deletion Policy (Account Deletion)

You can request deletion of your BiteCal account and associated personal data directly in the app:
Settings → Delete Account → Delete.

You can also contact support at:
support@viraldevelopment.co

What happens after you request deletion
We process account deletion in two phases:

1. Immediate lock & de-identification (usually within seconds): your account is locked, identifiers such as email, Google profile identifier, display name, and profile photo are removed, and you are signed out.

2. Background purge (batch deletion): we delete remaining app data in batches, including food photos, derived nutrition results, profile data, activity summaries, water data, workout data, weight history, notifications, quota counters, referral code records, and authentication tokens. This process may take additional time depending on queue load and retry conditions.

Retention schedule

 ● Draft/Pending/Failed food logs: retained up to 3 days, then photos and results are automatically deleted.

 ● Saved food logs: retained up to 32 days, then photos and saved nutrition history are automatically deleted.

 ● Account deletion requests: retained as a deletion audit record.

Billing and subscriptions
If you purchased a subscription or started a free trial through Google Play, your subscription is managed by Google Play.

Deleting your BiteCal account removes or de-identifies your app account and associated app data, but it may not automatically cancel your active Google Play subscription or free trial. Before continuing with account deletion, you can manage or cancel your subscription in Google Play:
Google Play → Payments & subscriptions → Subscriptions

For billing, refund handling, chargeback handling, fraud prevention, customer support, tax/accounting, and legal compliance, we may retain limited subscription records for a reasonable period. These retained records are minimized and are not used to recreate your deleted app account.

Re-registering
If you delete your account and later sign up again using the same email, it will be treated as a new account with a new user ID. A Google Play subscription may still be restorable only when Google Play confirms the purchase remains valid.
